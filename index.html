ABOUT

Sachin Ramesh Tendulkar (born on 24 April 1973) is an Indian former international cricketer who captained the Indian national team. He is widely regarded as one of the greatest cricketers of all time, and is the holder of several world records, including being the all-time highest run-scorer in both ODI and Test cricket, receiving the most player of the match awards in international cricket, and being the only batsman to score 100 international centuries. Tendulkar was a Member of Parliament, Rajya Sabha by presidential nomination from 2012 to 2018.Tendulkar was born at the Nirmal Nursing Home in the Dadar neighbourhood of Bombay, Maharashtra on 24 April 1973 into a Maharastrian family. His father, Ramesh Tendulkar, was a Marathi-language novelist and poet while his mother, Rajni, worked in the insurance industry.

On 14 November 1987, at age 14, Tendulkar was selected to represent Bombay in the Ranji Trophy for the 1987â€“88 season, but he was not selected for the final eleven in any of the matches, though he was often used as a substitute fielder. A year later, on 11 December 1988, aged 15 years and 232 days, Tendulkar made his debut for Bombay against Gujarat at Wankhede Stadium and scored 100 (not out) in that match, making him the youngest Indian to score a century on debut in first-class cricket. He was selected to play for the team by Bombay captain Dilip Vengsarkar, who watched him play Kapil Dev in Wankhede Stadium's cricket practice nets, where the Indian team had come to play against the touring New Zealand team. Tendulkar followed this by scoring a century each in his Deodhar and Duleep Trophy debuts, which are also India's domestic cricket tournaments.

ACHIEVEMENTS

Highest Run Scorer in International cricket

Tendulkar Holds the record for the most runs in international cricket, with 34,357 runs across all formats. He also holdes the record for the most centuries in international cricket, with 100 centuries in total.

Only Player to Score 100 International centuries

Sachin Tendulkar is the only cricketer in history to score 100 international centuries. He reached this milestone in March 2012, when he scored a century againist Banglasdesh in an Asia Cup match.

World Cup Winner

Tendulkar was a key member of the indian team that won the 2011 ICC Cricket World Cup. He scored 482 runs in the tournament, including two cneturies, and was named the player of tournament.

Wisden Cricketer of the Year

Tendulkar was named one of the Wisden Cricketers of the year in 1997. This is one of the highest honors in cricket, and tendulkar was the first Indian to recieve the award in over a decade.

Highest Run Scorer in World Cup History

Tendulkar is the highest run scorer in the history of cricket world cup, with 2,2778 runs in 45 matches, at an average of 56.95.

Most Test Runs

Tendulkar has scored the most runs in Test Cricket, with 15,921 runs in 200 matches. He is also one of only Five players to score over 10,000 runs in both Test and ODI cricket.

AWARDS

THE ARJUNA AWARD

This is India's second-highest sporting honor, awarded for outstanding achievement in sports. Tendulkar was awarded the Arjuna Award by the government of India in 1994

KHEL RATNA AWARD

Rajiv Gandhi Khel Ratna Award. This is India's highest sporting honor, awarded for excellence in sports performance. Awarded in 1997

PADMA SHRI

This is India's fourth-highest civilian award, awarded for distinguished service in any field. Awarded in 1999

PADMA VIBUSHAN

This is India's second-highest civilian award, awarded for exceptional and distinguished service. Awarded in 2008

BHARAT RATNA

This is India's highest civilian award, and Sachin Tendulkar became the first sportsperson and youngest person ever to receive this award. Awarded in 2014
